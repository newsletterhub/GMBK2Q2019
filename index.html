Compass Brooklyn 2Q2019 Market Report Inside
Following a similar dynamic to the Manhattan market, buyers continued to exercise their muscle in Brooklyn in the 2Q 2019 as evidenced by the highest average marketwide discount (7.4%) seen in years. If properties didn't sell in the first 59 days, they tended to linger on the market. North Brooklyn was the best performing submarket as price per square foot held steady ($1,329/sf) and price discounts on average were smaller (5%). Reflecting both the drop in mortgage rates and robust NYC economy, we anticipate to see more accelerated buying activity moving forward.
 
Highlights are as follows:
 
  • There were 2,588 recorded or closed sales in the 2Q 2019, a 9% increase from 2Q 2018. The borough wide discount was 7.4%, its highest 2Q-level since 2011.
     
  • Sales in the $500K-$1M price segment represented the largest share of closings with 47% of all deals, the highest of any quarter since 2009. Properties in this group averaged $731/sf and 1,290 square feet. The median and average prices fell slightly, both declining 1% to $725,000 and $739,642, respectively. The average price discount was 7%.
     
  • South Brooklyn commanded 51% of all transactions and had the largest average price discount of 9%. Prices changed slightly in the area, while average PPSF increased 6% and average size increased 7%.
 
  • There were 1,992 contracts signed across all of Brooklyn in the 2Q, a 4% increase from the same period last year.
     
  • Listings were fast to sell, but only in the first 59 days. After reaching that threshold, properties tended to stagnate with the fewest number of sales happening between 60 and 179 days.
 
 
  • Contracts priced between $500K-$1M comprised 35% of all deals, the most of any category. Those priced between $1M-$2M followed with 29%, while those less than $500K made up 20%. Contracts above $3M captured a 5% share, while the $2M-$3M segment took 11%.
     
  • North Brooklyn accounted for 15% of all deals and featured 41% larger properties compared to the second quarter last year. Accordingly, this area's median price increased 35% to $1,550,000 and average price increased 39% to $1,781,844, while average PPSF changed only slightly.
     
  • Northwest Brooklyn had 31% of all contracts and saw its median price decline 3% to $1,255,000 and its average price decline 8% to $1,624,238 from this time last year. The area's average PPSF and average size increased and decreased, respectively by 1%.
     
  • There were 5,478 active listed properties on the market at the end of the second quarter. Houses and townhouses comprised the largest share of inventory, with 41% of all active units. They had an average PPSF of $766 and an average size of 2,551 square feet.
     
  • Condos comprised 32% of all inventory and were the most expensive property type, averaging $1,057/sf. They measured an average of 1,170 square feet. Co-ops, with 27% of inventory, were the most affordable, averaging $677/sf and 1,086 square feet.
     
  • Despite an increase in activity in regard to both contracts signed and closed transactions in the first quarter, overall active listings increased to 5,478 units (vs. 4,159 in the 2Q 2018) and months of supply to 8.3 (vs. 7.3 in the 2Q 2018). All market indicators demonstrate that buyers will continue to have leverage in the foreseeable future.

    (Note: Months of supply represents the number of months it would take to absorb all active inventory based on a trailing 12 months average of contract activity. Typically 6 to 9 months of supply indicates market equilibrium. Please note this analysis does not include 'shadow inventory' of unsold, unlisted new development inventory.)​
